For Non-furries

New to the community, or just wondering what it's all about?

What is a furry?

Furries are anyone who is interested in animal characters—like from video games, books, or shows. Think of Disney's Zootopia, Puss in Boots, and Sonic the Hedgehog. Furries often create their own original characters, called fursonas, which represent them in a fun and creative way.

At its core, being a furry is about having fun, expressing yourself, and connecting with others who share an interest in cool animal characters!

What do furries do?

Our shared interests bring us together in many forms: art-making, sharing media, watching films/movies, and crafting costumes and cosplay. Our events include game nights, study jams, VR hangouts, and festivals with hundreds of attendees—taking place across San Diego, LA, and beyond.

We hope to showcase the best of what the fandom has to offer: a place for community, belonging, and having fun, whether or not you're a furry.

Common Myths & FAQs

Here are some common questions we get from non-furries:

Nope -- Many of our attendees are furry-adjacent, non-furries, or just curious about the fandom. You can come to most of our events even if you are not a furry.

Nope -- Many of our attendees are from the greater San Diego area or beyond! You can come to most of our events even if you are not a student.

Furries often cosplay as their character to be silly, but you definitely don't need one!

Fursuits are considered a luxury item -- Only about 25% of furries have a fursuit. Partial suits are at least $1,000-$2,000, while full suits are at least $3,000-$5,000.

Furries often create original characters, known as "fursonas," as a unique form of self-expression. This can be more challenging with just human characters or typical media. It's a fun and creative process, similar to how sports teams have mascots, how you choose your profile picture online, or how you customize your avatar in online games.

A close analogy would be asking, "What is your spirit animal?" and then developing that concept into a character that embodies your personality, values, and beliefs.

Furries are not inherently NSFW — This is a common misconception and fictional portrayal of the fandom.

Triton Tails does not and will not allow such content or conduct in the community, as it is completely out of line with our values and not representative of what we stand for.

Triton Tails is the UC San Diego chapter of the AAAA (Anthropomorphic Animal Appreciation Association), the global furry network for universities. There are over 100 chapters, so it's pretty likely to find one at a nearby university! Our extensive alumni network connects students with professionals, faculty, and employers across various industries and academia worldwide.
